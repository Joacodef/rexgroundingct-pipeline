Master Plan — ReXGroundingCT Challenge 2026
Primary Goal: Top-3 on the leaderboard (September 2026) AND original paper accepted at MICCAI 2026.
Milestone 1: June 1, 2026. Goal: **`>0.30 Average Dice`** in local validation + tested submission pipeline + running methodological architecture.
Methodological Strategy (The Paper's "Contribution")
The training dataset has partial annotations (maximum of 3 instances per finding), whereas validation and test phases require exhaustive segmentation. To leverage this without being penalized by models that assume complete masks:
Baseline: VoxTell v1.0 (Frozen Text Encoder, decoder fine-tuning).
Strong Supervision (ROIs): Apply DiceCE loss only within dilated regions around the annotated instances (adapting the SPOCO principle from Wolny et al. 2022).
Weak Supervision (Unannotated regions): Implement a Mean Teacher framework. Apply MPR loss (SOUSA, Gao et al. 2022) over 3D max projections to penalize student false positives in unannotated areas, ensuring spatial consistency.
Operational Constraints and Considerations (Multi-Server Environment)
The pipeline is deployed across three environments: Jumbito (compute.pln.villena.cl) and two SLURM-managed clusters.
Execution and Persistence: 
- On Jumbito: In the absence of a queue manager, there is no job time limit. Long runs must use tmux (preferred over screen).
- On SLURM clusters: Jobs are subject to queue wait times and strict wall-clock limits. The training loop must include checkpoint saving and resumption to recover from preemptions.
I/O and Storage Management: Massive 4D NIfTI processing must be isolated in fast, local filesystems (/tmp on Jumbito; node-local scratch like $SLURM_TMPDIR on SLURM). Temporary filesystems have automated deletion policies, so final artifacts must be synchronized to permanent storage at the end of every run.
Execution Environment: Use `uv` and `pyenv` for strict dependency isolation. If containerizing, note that SLURM clusters typically require Apptainer/Singularity, whereas Jumbito supports rootless Docker.
Multi-GPU Control (DDP): Resource allocation must be manual using CUDA_VISIBLE_DEVICES. To avoid the Address already in use socket error when other users run distributed tasks, a dynamic or non-default MASTER_PORT (other than 29500) must be assigned in the launch scripts.
Submission Format: Mandatory 4D NIfTI () identical to the original ground truth. Post-inference stacking must be robust.
Critical Timeline up to Milestone 1 (June 1)
Week 1: Securing Baseline and Submission
Local Setup: Set up the environment with frozen dependencies on jumbito. Configure SSH tunnels (ssh -L) for remote access to Jupyter/VS Code and monitoring.
Preprocessing & Resolution: Bypassed/deprecated 1.5mm resampling (which severely degrades VoxTell v1.1 metrics to ~0.08). Ensure execution runs strictly in native CT resolution.
Spatial Alignment (4D Back-Reorientation): Address the Identity Affine metadata bug in the Ground Truth segmentations. Do not evaluate in RAS directly; apply a 4D Back-Reorientation to map model predictions back into the tomographies' original coordinate space before saving.
Zero-shot Inference: Run native resolution batch inference with VoxTell v1.1, achieving a strong baseline of ~0.2138 Dice score and ~48.70% Hit Rate.
Format Validation: Confirm that predictions are saved as 4D NIfTIs perfectly matching the Ground Truth spatial shape and affine parameters.
Week 2: Methodological Development
Mean Teacher: Integrate the teacher model's EMA update into the VoxTell training loop.
Masked Loss: Modify the loss function to calculate DiceCE only on dilated masks of known positives.
3D Consistency (MPR): Implement max projection calculations and the loss between teacher and student in regions outside the ROIs.
Sanity Checks: Train on a mini-batch to confirm that losses decrease and tensors flow without errors.
Week 3: Fine-tuning at Scale
DDP Launch: Start distributed training in tmux, managing CUDA_VISIBLE_DEVICES and the MASTER_PORT explicitly.
Monitoring: Monitor curves in Weights & Biases (access via SSH tunnel if necessary). If the Dice score crosses 0.30 on the local validation proxy, freeze weights, move artifacts out of /tmp, and prepare the submission.
Post-Launch Challenge Phase (June - September)
Official Evaluation: Send the first submission as soon as the 200 validation cases are released. Compare local Dice vs. official Dice.
Ablation Studies: Train variants by disabling the consistency loss or varying the weights to test the methodological impact.
Ensembling: Combine the hybrid model with standard fine-tuned versions or average multi-scale inferences.
